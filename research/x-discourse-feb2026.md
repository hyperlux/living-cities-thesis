## Target Account Analysis

### @DrorPoleg - The Jobless Boom Thesis

**Key Insight (Feb 18, 2026)**: "Welcome to the Jobless Boom"

Dror Poleg's latest work represents perhaps the most contrarian and data-backed take in this space:

> "The economy grew 2.7% in 2025. Employment barely grew at all... US real GDP and nonfarm payrolls — two lines that moved in near-lockstep for over three decades — are diverging in a way that 'has never happened this far into expansion.'"

**Structural argument**: This isn't a lag, it's a new production function.
- OpenAI: <700 employees → first $1B revenue
- Anthropic: 2,500 employees → $14B revenue run rate  
- Google in 2004: 2,500 employees → $1B revenue

**Implications for cities**:
- The NAIOP model that predicted office demand for decades is now off by 90%
- Cities need to become "as flexible and amorphous as the economic activity they contain"
- Proposed: Cities should treat innovation profits like Norway treats oil revenue - tax them meaningfully but allocate to independent sovereign wealth funds

**Political manifestation**: 
- San Francisco elected reform mayor embracing zoning liberalization
- NYC elected populist socialist because "the economy was growing, the stock market was soaring, and yet their material lives weren't getting better"

**Contrarian take**: The "jobless boom" isn't a bug to be fixed - it's a structural reality requiring fundamental reimagining of cities' economic role.

### @balajis / Balaji Srinivasan - Network State Evolution

**Core thesis** from The Network State:

> "A network state is a highly aligned online community with a capacity for collective action that crowdfunds territory around the world and eventually gains diplomatic recognition from pre-existing states."

**Key distinction**: Network states require moral innovation, not just regulatory arbitrage.

> "Early America's religious colonies succeeded at a higher rate than its for-profit colonies, because the former had a purpose... libertarian attempts at micronations are like 'Zionism without Judaism', and this is a key part of why they fail."

**Critique of current approaches**:
- Too focused on exit, not enough on building
- "NYT, CCP, BTC" trichotomy - all three teams are too nationalist and self-interested
- Network states should be a "de-centralized center" combining freedom (BTC), moral energy (NYT), and organization (CCP)

**Emerging project**: Network School (ns.com) - attempting to instantiate these principles at small scale.

## Emerging Projects Actually Shipping

### Praxis Nation
**Status**: 151,068 citizens across 82 countries

> "The World's First Digital Nation... Our purpose is to restore Western Civilization and pursue our ultimate destiny of life among the stars."

**Pivot noted**: Shifted from "Mediterranean renaissance" to "American military dynamism" (Atlas California proposal)

**Critique**: More parties in upscale US cities than actual city-building (per Vitalik's observations)

### Culdesac Tempe
**Actually built and operating**:
- Car-free development in Arizona
- 21 local businesses, 50+ courtyards
- Transportation partnerships with Waymo, Bird, metro
- Proving walkable communities can work in car-centric America

### Cabin DAO (Wound Down 2025)
**Critical lessons from failure**:

> "Cabin showed that internet-native communities can instantiate in the real world... After four years of operation, the community voted to wind down the formal DAO structure in 2025."

**Why it failed**:
- "Operational overhead proved heavier than the network could sustain"
- Difficulty scaling coordination at frontier
- Balance between "idealism and practical logistics"

**What remains**: Neighborhood Village Project spun out, continuing in 100+ communities. Dense web of relationships persisting.

### Prospera (Honduras)
**Status**: ~100 population, beautiful physical venue, Hondurans integrated into leadership

**Limitation noted** (Vitalik): "One restaurant with limited food options and no other amenities within walking distance" - needs 1-2 more growth stages.

## Critical Takes & Contrarian Perspectives

### Vitalik Buterin's "Let a Thousand Societies Bloom" (Dec 2025)

**On popup fatigue**:
> "Over time, popups would get shorter in duration, smaller in scope, and more generic in substance, to the point where in the limit they approach being simply a few more conferences and hackerspaces."

**On governance innovation retreat**:
> "Two things core to early ideology often fall away over time: novel governance designs, and a search for legal autonomy... within the context of popups, this makes total sense. If a popup is short-duration, then 'forking as governance' works perfectly fine."

**Minimum viable city sizes**:
- Longyearbyen (northernmost settlement): 2,600 people sustains 10 restaurants, airport, hospital, school
- University towns: 30,000-150,000
- Specialized sport towns: 1,000-10,000
- 100 people (like Prospera): "probably not enough"

### Vitalik on Network States (2022, still relevant critique)

**The "zone of autonomy" critique**:

## Synthesis: Key Tensions in Feb 2026 Discourse

### 1. Exit vs. Voice
- **Exit camp**: Balaji, Seasteading, Praxis - build new rather than fix old
- **Voice camp**: GovLab, Strong Towns - reform existing institutions
- **Hybrid**: Vitalik's "tribes with hubs in zones" - work with existing states while building culture

### 2. Grand Vision vs. Incremental Shipping
- Grand visions (network states, seasteads) attract attention but struggle to materialize
- Incremental projects (Culdesac, Square Roots) actually operating
- Zuzalu represents middle path: ambitious experiments, limited duration

### 3. Tech Solutionism vs. Cultural Rootedness
- Early network state thinking: crypto + governance tech = new society
- Evolved thinking: "Zionism without Judaism" fails - need moral/cultural core
- Balaji's insight: "What start off as theories held by a few people may come to infiltrate the social imaginary"

### 4. Jobless Future vs. Labor-Centric Planning
- Dror Poleg: cities still designed for employment patterns that no longer exist
- Most city planning assumes job growth → population growth → housing demand
- What happens when economic output decouples from headcount?

### 5. Autonomy vs. Coordination
- Pure autonomy seekers (Liberland, radical seasteads) remain marginal
- Successful projects negotiate with existing states (Prospera, SEZs)
- Vitalik: "All new countries are forged in negotiation with traditional countries"
