## Circular Fashion & Textile Waste 2025

**Scale of Problem:**
- Fashion industry linear model causing environmental/social challenges
- Overproduction and overconsumption
- International Day of Zero Waste 2025 focused on textiles

**EU Legislation (Sept 2025):**
- "EU laws for sustainable textile consumption"
- Reduce textile waste
- Increase lifecycle and recycling
- Improving sustainability

**Economic Opportunity (BCG Aug 2025):**
- "From Waste to Worth"
- $150 billion value to reclaim
- Waste recycling rates could exceed 30%
- New fibers with $50 billion raw material value
- ~180,000 new jobs potential

**Textile-to-Textile Recycling:**
- "Nearing Mainstream Adoption"
- Major setbacks (Renewcell bankruptcy)
- But cautious optimism growing
- Technology advancing

**Academic Review (ScienceDirect):**
- Circular economy principles for fashion/textiles
- Current waste generation examined
- Management strategies assessed
- Sustainable practices needed

**Key Framing:** Clothing waste is real pollution — textiles can be circular.

**Definition:**
- Streets temporarily closed to cars
- Children play freely
- Community gathering space
- Safe zones for families

**London Play Streets:**
- Car Free Day 2025
- "Witness the change when you open your street for play"

- Kids chalk on road, community fun

- "Safe spaces to gather, children room to play"

**Westminster (Sept 2025):**
- "New Play Streets Bring Families Together"

**Stuttgart Action Plan:**

## Synthesis: Materials + Play + Design for Living Cities

| Domain | Linear/Car Model | Living City Model |
|--------|-----------------|------------------|
| Clothing | Buy, wear, discard | Circular, repair, recycle |
| Streets | Cars dominate | Children play |
| Design | Ignore nature | Learn from nature |

**Living City Design:**

1. **Circular Textiles:**
   - Clothing library/sharing
   - Repair and alteration services
   - Textile-to-textile recycling
   - No fast fashion
   - Local production from recycled fibers

2. **Play Streets:**
   - Regular play street days
   - Some streets permanently car-free
   - Children's right to outdoor play
   - Community gathering enabled
   - Safe movement for all ages

3. **Biomimicry Design:**
   - Water systems like watersheds
   - Air flow like termite mounds
   - Materials like shells and bones
   - Networks like mycelium
   - Resilience like ecosystems

**Metric:**
- Textile waste per capita
- % of streets with regular play time
- Biomimicry principles adopted in design guidelines
