## 2. AI Agent Governance Experiments

### 2.1 Collective Constitutional AI (Anthropic + Collective Intelligence Project)

**Landmark experiment completed 2023-2024**

**Methodology:**
- ~1,000 Americans participated via Polis platform
- Public drafted AI constitution through deliberation
- 1,127 statements contributed, 38,252 votes cast
- High consensus found on most statements

**Key Findings:**
- ~50% overlap between public and Anthropic-written constitutions
- Public constitution was MORE focused on:
  - Objectivity and impartiality
  - Accessibility (especially for disabilities)
  - Promoting positive behavior (vs. avoiding negative)
  - Self-generated principles (not copied from existing documents)

**Model Training Results:**
- Public-constitution model performed equivalently on language/math tasks
- Rated equally helpful and harmless by users
- **Lower bias** across 9 social dimensions (especially disability status, physical appearance)
- Similar political ideology representation (slightly liberal-leaning for both)

**Implications:**
- Demonstrates feasibility of democratic AI alignment
- Public preferences differ meaningfully from developer assumptions
- Deliberation can produce coherent, trainable constitutional principles

### 2.2 OpenAI Democratic Inputs to AI Program

**Grant program launched 2023**

**Core Questions Posed:**
- Under what conditions should AI criticize public figures?
- How should disputed views be represented?
- Should AI reflect median global, national, or demographic persona?

**Program Design:**
- Sought global teams to develop proof-of-concepts
- Focus on deliberation, diverse representation, avoiding participation washing
- Results intended as stepping stones, not binding decisions

**Key Design Considerations:**
- Participant selection methodology
- Handling of minority vs. majority group representation
- Domain expertise requirements
- Decision-making algorithms: elected representatives, majority voting, liquid democracy, sortition

**Explicit Goals:**
- Strong public oversight of powerful AI systems
- Complement (not substitute) government regulation
- Build novel democratic tools for future binding decisions

## 3. Community Land Trust Innovations

### 3.1 CLT Model Overview (Grounded Solutions Network)

**How CLTs Work:**
- Nonprofit owns land; family purchases house only
- 99-year renewable ground lease
- Restricted resale price maintains permanent affordability
- Homeowners can realize appreciation from improvements

**Governance:**
- Board includes: CLT residents, community residents, public representatives
- Balances resident interests, community needs, and public interest

**Applications Beyond Housing:**
- Urban/rural agriculture
- Commercial spaces for local businesses
- Affordable rental and cooperative housing
- Land conservation and urban green spaces

**Scale (2022 Census):**
- Significant growth in CLT numbers and shared equity programs
- Grounded Solutions Network provides technical manual and startup toolkit

---

### 3.2 Key Innovations & Trends

**Model Extensions:**
- Commercial CLTs for small business affordability
- Mixed-use CLTs combining housing with community facilities
- Agricultural CLTs for food sovereignty

**Policy Integration:**
- Inclusionary zoning partnerships
- Public land disposition to CLTs
- HUD/state funding streams

**Technical Resources:**
- Start-up CLT Hub toolkit
- Community Land Trust Technical Manual
- 2022 Census providing comprehensive data

## 4. Participatory Budgeting at Scale

### 4.1 Global Growth

**Scale (2024):**
- **11,500+ municipal PB processes globally** (up from ~1,500 in 2014)
- Originated in Porto Alegre, Brazil (1989)
- Now implemented across Americas, Africa, Asia, Europe

**Documented Outcomes:**
- More equitable public spending
- Greater government transparency
- Increased public participation (especially marginalized groups)
- Reduced infant mortality (Brazilian studies)
- Improved public services (NYC 311 call reduction)

---

### 4.2 Digital Participatory Budgeting

**Key Platforms:**

**Decidim** (Barcelona-origin)
- Free/libre open source platform
- Used by hundreds of organizations globally
- Features: strategic planning, assemblies, initiatives, networked communication
- Version 0.31.0 released November 2025 (Rails 7.2 upgrade)

**Polis**
- Used for deliberation phase of PB
- Identifies consensus and opinion clusters

**Technology Evolution:**
- Online voting pioneered in Ipatinga, Brazil (2001)
- Expanded to Rio Grande do Sul (2003), Belo Horizonte (2006)
- Now standard in Paris, NYC, Lisbon, Madrid, Mexico City

**Research Findings:**
- Online voting increased turnout 8.2% in Rio Grande do Sul
- Attracts younger, more educated participants online
- No systematic difference in vote choices between online/offline voters

### 4.4 Challenges & Evolution

**Known Failure Modes:**
- Inadequate minority/majority representation
- Manipulation by special interests
- Insufficiently informed participants
- "Participation washing"

**Structural Gaps:**
- Most PB focuses on formulation/debate phases
- Execution and control stages often lack citizen oversight
- Bureaucratic actors retain key decision power

**Porto Alegre Decline:**
- PB suspended by current city leadership (as of 2017)
- Political support declined over time
- Cautionary tale about sustainability without institutional commitment

### 5.3 Emerging Tensions

1. **Sovereignty vs. Democracy:** Network states often prioritize exit over voice; charter cities grant developer veto power
2. **Crypto Dependency:** Multiple projects rely on volatile digital assets for funding
3. **Scale vs. Participation:** Larger PB/governance systems struggle to maintain deliberative quality
4. **Cultural Framing:** Projects like Praxis use civilizational/Western restoration framing that may limit appeal
5. **Legal Uncertainty:** Network states operate in legal grey zones; Pr√≥spera case may set precedents

### 5.4 Key Innovations to Watch

1. **Bhutan's GNH-integrated development model** ‚Äî Alternative to libertarian charter city paradigm
2. **Constitutional AI via public deliberation** ‚Äî Scalable democratic AI alignment
3. **Commercial CLTs** ‚Äî Extending affordability model beyond housing
4. **Decidim ecosystem** ‚Äî Open-source democratic infrastructure
5. **Hybrid digital/physical governance** ‚Äî Praxis's "digital nation" concept evolution
