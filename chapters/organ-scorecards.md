## 1. NOURISHMENT ORGAN
### Food Systems Diagnostic Scorecard

**Biological Principle:** Every cell needs nutrients delivered reliably. The nourishment organ ensures all parts of the organism receive sustenance without toxicity or deprivation.

**Post-Labor Function:** When work no longer guarantees food access, the nourishment organ ensures universal nutrition through ownership mechanisms rather than employment-based purchasing power.

### Warning Signs of Organ Failure

**Early Stage (Subclinical):**
- Farmers markets closing in low-income areas
- School lunch participation declining
- Food bank demand increasing >10% year-over-year
- Single grocery chain dominates >60% of market

**Mid Stage (Symptomatic):**
- "Food deserts" expanding geographically
- Hospital admissions for malnutrition increasing
- Local farms converting to non-agricultural use
- Food prices rising faster than wages

**Late Stage (Critical):**
- Widespread food insecurity (>25% of households)
- Emergency food systems permanently overwhelmed
- No local food production within 50 miles
- Diet-related disease is leading cause of preventable death

---

### Attractor Design Patterns

**Pattern 1: Community Food Ownership Cooperative**
- **Structure:** Resident-owned grocery cooperative with democratic governance
- **Incentive Alignment:** Members pay wholesale, share in surplus, vote on sourcing
- **Anti-Capture Mechanism:** Membership capped; excess profits fund new locations in underserved areas
- **Existing Models:** Park Slope Food Coop (Brooklyn), Weaver Street Market (NC), Co-op Grocery Network

**Pattern 2: Municipal Food Resilience Trust**
- **Structure:** City-owned strategic food reserve + local farm investment fund
- **Incentive Alignment:** Tax increment financing from improved food access areas funds reserve
- **Anti-Capture Mechanism:** Rotating board with farmer, consumer, public health seats; sunset review every 5 years
- **Existing Models:** Singapore food security stockpile, Cuba urban agriculture system

**Pattern 3: Food System Targeting Authority**
- **Structure:** Independent body sets food access targets, posts escrowed bounties
- **Incentive Alignment:** Grocery providers paid per household gaining fresh food access
- **Anti-Capture Mechanism:** Blinded verification; red-team bounties for gaming
- **Target Example:** "$10M escrowed. Released when 95% of addresses have fresh produce within 10 min walk."

**Pattern 4: Universal Food Dividend**
- **Structure:** Per-resident allocation for food purchasing, funded by land value capture
- **Incentive Alignment:** Markets still function; residents choose where to shop
- **Anti-Capture Mechanism:** Transferable within food category only; expires if unspent
- **Existing Models:** SNAP (current), but universalized and funded by local ownership

**Post-Labor Function:** When energy is the primary input to automated production, democratic ownership of metabolism determines whether abundance flows to all or concentrates.

### Warning Signs of Organ Failure

**Early Stage:**
- Electricity prices rising faster than inflation
- Solar/battery deployment concentrated only in wealthy areas
- Single utility controls >90% of generation
- "Sacrifice zones" emerging with concentrated pollution

**Mid Stage:**
- Energy poverty affecting >15% of households (choosing between heat and food)
- Grid instability during peak demand
- Out-of-state ownership extracting profits
- Renewable projects blocked by incumbent interests

**Late Stage:**
- Blackouts affecting critical services
- Mass fuel poverty, health impacts
- Complete dependence on imported energy
- Climate vulnerability without adaptive capacity

---

### Attractor Design Patterns

**Pattern 1: Community Energy Cooperative**
- **Structure:** Member-owned renewable generation and storage
- **Incentive Alignment:** Members receive dividends from energy sales; surplus funds expansion
- **Anti-Capture Mechanism:** Geographic membership requirements; one member one vote
- **Existing Models:** Middelgrunden (Denmark), Hepburn Wind (Australia), RECs (rural US)

**Pattern 2: Metabolic Citizenship Energy Account**
- **Structure:** Every resident receives energy allocation from community-owned generation
- **Incentive Alignment:** Use what you need, sell surplus back; everyone has baseline security
- **Anti-Capture Mechanism:** Personal caps prevent hoarding; commercial users pay market rates
- **Existing Models:** Alaska Permanent Fund (conceptual model), community solar credits

**Pattern 3: Circular Economy Targeting Authority**
- **Structure:** Sets material circularity targets, pays for verified waste diversion
- **Incentive Alignment:** Businesses earn bounties for circular design; landfills pay escalating fees
- **Anti-Capture Mechanism:** Independent lifecycle assessment; materials tracking via blockchain
- **Target Example:** "$20M escrowed. Released when city achieves 60% material circularity rate."

**Pattern 4: Grid Democracy Protocol**
- **Structure:** Distributed energy resources governed by prosumer assembly
- **Incentive Alignment:** Prosumers set grid rules collectively; automated market clearing
- **Anti-Capture Mechanism:** Minimum participation requirements; algorithmic price stabilization
- **Existing Models:** Brooklyn Microgrid pilot, European energy communities

**Post-Labor Function:** When work no longer determines where you must travel, circulation becomes about connection, access, and participation rather than commuting to employment.

### Warning Signs of Organ Failure

**Early Stage:**
- Bus routes being cut in low-income areas
- Traffic fatalities increasing
- Average commute time increasing year-over-year
- Ride-share capturing >30% of trips with no worker protections

**Mid Stage:**
- Transit "death spiral" (cuts â†’ fewer riders â†’ more cuts)
- Traffic congestion causing measurable health impacts
- Car ownership required for employment access
- Logistics chokepoints causing supply chain failures

**Late Stage:**
- Large areas with no transit service
- Traffic fatalities among leading causes of death
- >30% of income going to transportation for low-income households
- Economic activity constrained by circulation failure

---

### Attractor Design Patterns

**Pattern 1: Transit Rider Cooperative**
- **Structure:** Rider-owned transit system with democratic governance
- **Incentive Alignment:** Riders vote on routes/schedules; efficiency gains return as service or dividends
- **Anti-Capture Mechanism:** Geographic representation requirements; operating subsidies tied to equity metrics
- **Existing Models:** Some European transit mutuals, worker-owned transit (Argentina recovered factories)

**Pattern 2: Mobility-as-Commons Authority**
- **Structure:** Public body integrates all modes into seamless, fare-integrated network
- **Incentive Alignment:** Providers paid per passenger-mile; mode-agnostic optimization
- **Anti-Capture Mechanism:** Open data requirements; interoperability mandates
- **Existing Models:** Helsinki MaaS, Vienna mobility card, Seoul integration

**Pattern 3: 15-Minute City Targeting Authority**
- **Structure:** Sets access targets by neighborhood, funds interventions
- **Incentive Alignment:** Developers earn density bonuses for meeting access targets
- **Anti-Capture Mechanism:** Third-party verification; community veto on changes that reduce access
- **Target Example:** "$50M escrowed. Released when 90% of residents reach 10 destinations in 15 min."

**Pattern 4: Logistics Commons**
- **Structure:** Shared last-mile delivery infrastructure owned by participating businesses
- **Incentive Alignment:** Reduces individual delivery costs; consolidation reduces traffic
- **Anti-Capture Mechanism:** Open membership; standard APIs; cap on any single user
- **Existing Models:** Urban consolidation centers (Europe), cargo bike cooperatives

### Warning Signs of Organ Failure

**Early Stage:**
- Median rent increasing faster than median income for 3+ consecutive years
- New construction concentrated only in luxury segment
- Community land trusts losing land to speculation
- Eviction filings increasing

**Mid Stage:**
- Essential workers unable to live in city they serve
- Neighborhood turnover causing cultural displacement
- Vacant units held for speculation while waitlists grow
- Informal housing/overcrowding increasing

**Late Stage:**
- Mass homelessness visible on streets
- Middle class priced out entirely
- Housing abandonment in some areas while scarcity in others
- Housing instability causing cascading organ failures (health, education, safety)

---

### Attractor Design Patterns

**Pattern 1: Community Land Trust Network**
- **Structure:** Permanently affordable land held in community trust; buildings owned by residents
- **Incentive Alignment:** Land value captured for community; residents build equity in buildings
- **Anti-Capture Mechanism:** Resale restrictions; community board governance; perpetual affordability
- **Existing Models:** Champlain Housing Trust (VT), Dudley Neighbors (Boston), UK CLT network

**Pattern 2: Social Housing Investment Fund**
- **Structure:** City-controlled fund builds/acquires housing, rents below market, reinvests surplus
- **Incentive Alignment:** Land value capture funds acquisition; good management reduces costs
- **Anti-Capture Mechanism:** Independent board; tenant representation; operating covenant permanence
- **Existing Models:** Vienna social housing, Singapore HDB, Montreal affordable housing trust

**Pattern 3: Tenant Opportunity-to-Purchase (TOPA) + Cooperative Conversion**
- **Structure:** Tenants get first right to buy building when sold; financing for cooperative conversion
- **Incentive Alignment:** Long-term tenants become owners; building stability improves
- **Anti-Capture Mechanism:** Anti-flipping restrictions; cooperative governance requirements
- **Existing Models:** Washington DC TOPA, Berlin's tenant buyback program

**Pattern 4: Speculation Tax + Vacancy Penalty**
- **Structure:** Progressive tax on rapid resale and long-term vacancy
- **Incentive Alignment:** Holding housing as speculation becomes unprofitable; supply increases
- **Anti-Capture Mechanism:** Revenue dedicated to affordable housing fund; exemptions for residents
- **Existing Models:** Vancouver speculation tax, Barcelona tourist housing limits

### Attractor Design Patterns

**Pattern 1: Citizen Data Cooperative**
- **Structure:** Resident-owned collective for personal/civic data with democratic governance
- **Incentive Alignment:** Members receive dividends from ethical data use; control access terms
- **Anti-Capture Mechanism:** One member one vote; data portability rights; audit transparency
- **Existing Models:** MIDATA (Switzerland), Salus.coop (Barcelona), Driver's Seat Cooperative

**Pattern 2: Decidim-Style Participatory Platform**
- **Structure:** Open-source platform for proposals, deliberation, voting, and accountability tracking
- **Incentive Alignment:** Politicians gain legitimacy from participation; citizens see impact
- **Anti-Capture Mechanism:** Open source; community governance; mandatory response to reaching quorum
- **Existing Models:** Decidim (Barcelona), Consul (Madrid), vTaiwan

**Pattern 3: Algorithmic Accountability Board**
- **Structure:** Independent body audits algorithmic decision-making in city services
- **Incentive Alignment:** Agencies must demonstrate fairness to use algorithms; audit findings public
- **Anti-Capture Mechanism:** Board includes technical and community members; rotating terms
- **Existing Models:** NYC Automated Decision Systems Task Force, Amsterdam Algorithm Register

**Pattern 4: Community Information Commons**
- **Structure:** Nonprofit local news + civic information infrastructure, community-owned
- **Incentive Alignment:** Diverse funding (membership, foundation, government) reduces capture
- **Anti-Capture Mechanism:** Firewall between funding and editorial; community advisory board
- **Existing Models:** Voice of San Diego, City Bureau (Chicago), Devil Strip (Akron model)

### Attractor Design Patterns

**Pattern 1: Community Safety Cooperative**
- **Structure:** Neighborhood-governed safety coordination with professional and volunteer elements
- **Incentive Alignment:** Community defines "safety"; resources flow to community-determined priorities
- **Anti-Capture Mechanism:** Participatory budgeting for safety; oversight with teeth
- **Existing Models:** Cure Violence (interrupters), Peace Circles, community mediation centers

**Pattern 2: Restorative Justice Default**
- **Structure:** RJ becomes standard for eligible offenses; traditional court is backup
- **Incentive Alignment:** Cheaper than incarceration; better outcomes for all parties
- **Anti-Capture Mechanism:** Victim choice preserved; RJ practitioner independence
- **Existing Models:** New Zealand youth justice, Navajo Peacemaking Courts

**Pattern 3: Crisis Response Unbundling**
- **Structure:** Non-police responders for mental health, homelessness, traffic, noise
- **Incentive Alignment:** Better outcomes at lower cost; responders matched to situation
- **Anti-Capture Mechanism:** Independent evaluation; community dispatch protocols
- **Existing Models:** CAHOOTS (Eugene), Denver STAR, Albuquerque ACS

**Pattern 4: System Defense Targeting Authority**
- **Structure:** Sets measurable safety and justice targets; posts escrowed bounties
- **Incentive Alignment:** Providers paid for verified safety improvements, not arrests
- **Anti-Capture Mechanism:** Outcome measurement by independent third party
- **Target Example:** "$15M escrowed. Released when violent crime drops 30% AND incarceration drops 20%."

### Attractor Design Patterns

**Pattern 1: Circular Economy Exchange**
- **Structure:** Platform matching waste producers with input seekers; industrial symbiosis
- **Incentive Alignment:** Waste becomes revenue; virgin material costs more than recycled
- **Anti-Capture Mechanism:** Open participation; material tracking; verified circular claims
- **Existing Models:** Kalundborg (Denmark), industrial symbiosis networks

**Pattern 2: Extended Producer Responsibility Mandate**
- **Structure:** Producers responsible for end-of-life management of products
- **Incentive Alignment:** Design for recyclability becomes profitable; planned obsolescence penalized
- **Anti-Capture Mechanism:** Independent verification; escalating fees for non-compliance
- **Existing Models:** EU packaging directive, BC EPR programs

**Pattern 3: Community Composting Cooperative**
- **Structure:** Neighborhood-scale composting with member participation and governance
- **Incentive Alignment:** Members get compost; surplus sold; keeps organics local
- **Anti-Capture Mechanism:** Membership caps; excess capacity funds new sites
- **Existing Models:** BIG Reuse (NYC), community composting networks

**Pattern 4: Regeneration Targeting Authority**
- **Structure:** Sets waste reduction and remediation targets; posts escrowed bounties
- **Incentive Alignment:** Innovators earn rewards for verified waste reduction
- **Anti-Capture Mechanism:** Lifecycle verification; third-party audits
- **Target Example:** "$25M escrowed. Released when per-capita waste drops 50% AND all brownfields enter remediation."

### Attractor Design Patterns

**Pattern 1: Third Place Network**
- **Structure:** Publicly-funded non-commercial gathering spaces in every neighborhood
- **Incentive Alignment:** Community determines programming; attendance is the metric
- **Anti-Capture Mechanism:** Cannot be privatized; must be free to enter; community governance
- **Existing Models:** Oodi Library (Helsinki), Men's Sheds (Australia), Community centers (Japan)

**Pattern 2: Contribution Recognition System**
- **Structure:** Status currencies that recognize non-market contributions
- **Incentive Alignment:** Contribution visible and honored; not converted to money
- **Anti-Capture Mechanism:** Multiple recognition paths; community-defined value
- **Existing Models:** Timebanking, citizen science recognition, volunteer honors

**Pattern 3: Ritual Infrastructure**
- **Structure:** Secular rituals marking life transitions, seasons, community events
- **Incentive Alignment:** Participation creates belonging; rituals create meaning
- **Anti-Capture Mechanism:** Community ownership of ritual design; pluralistic options
- **Existing Models:** Secular weddings/funerals, community festivals, solidarity events

**Pattern 4: Meaning Weaving Cooperative**
- **Structure:** Member-governed organization creating narrative, identity, connection
- **Incentive Alignment:** Members define what meaningful life looks like; services support it
- **Anti-Capture Mechanism:** No single ideology dominates; pluralism required
- **Target Example:** Community-governed gathering spaces, storytelling circles, mentorship networks

**Pattern 5: Generosity Infrastructure**
- **Structure:** Systems that make giving easy, visible, and normal
- **Incentive Alignment:** Giving feels good; recognition without scorekeeping
- **Anti-Capture Mechanism:** Multiple pathways; cannot be hoarded; flows by design
- **Example:** Mutual aid networks, care circles, skill-sharing, time banks

## APPENDIX B: GLOSSARY

**Attractor:** In complex systems, a state toward which the system naturally evolves. In governance, the incentive structures and feedback loops that pull behavior toward certain outcomes.

**Capture:** When a system meant to serve a broad public comes to serve a narrow interest (regulatory capture, platform capture, political capture).

**Circulation:** The flow of value through a system. Living systems require circulation; blocked flows cause death.

**Cooperative:** A member-owned and democratically governed organization, whether of workers, consumers, producers, or residents.

**Data Cooperative:** A member-owned organization that manages collective data, enables collective bargaining with data users, and distributes value back to member-producers.

**Living City:** An urban system that operates as an adaptive organism serving its residents, where the coordination layer is citizen-owned rather than platform-captured or state-controlled.

**Organ:** A functional subsystem of the city organism, analogous to biological organs (nourishment, metabolism, circulation, etc.).

**Post-Labor Economics:** Economic structures designed for a world where human labor is increasingly unnecessary for production, based on ownership rather than employment.

**Targeting Authority:** A body that defines measurable targets, posts escrowed bounties, and verifies outcomesâ€”creating market incentives for solving specific problems.

**Third Place:** A social space separate from home (first place) and work (second place) where community happensâ€”libraries, parks, cafes, community centers.
